World of Fantasy

A world of magical fantasy, where the wonders of magic roam the world, giving rise to different races with different customs and beliefs; some collaborate with each other and others are mortal enemies.

In our special world, nothing is too crazy and the strangest things become everyday occurrences. Now you will encounter different races and discover what makes them so special and much more about them.

Fairies️

When we experience the magical side of nature — the shimmering, incredible mystery of nature … we personify the dynamics as fairies.

Like so many “imaginary” parts of childhood, there exists a tension between the world of the creative child and the realistic adult. At some point these things must be reconciled. But that doesn’t mean we stop believing! Belief in fairies isn’t only for children. Sometimes we just need to remember how to imagine. My children know that I believe in fairies — because I do.

fairy, a mythical being of folklore and romance usually having magic powers and dwelling on earth in close relationship with humans. It can appear as a dwarf creature typically having green clothes and hair, living underground or in stone heaps, and characteristically exercising magic powers to benevolent ends; as a diminutive sprite commonly in the shape of a delicate, beautiful, ageless winged woman dressed in diaphanous white clothing, inhabiting fairyland, but making usually well-intentioned intervention in personal human affairs; or as a tiny, mischievous, and protective creature generally associated with a household hearth.

Types of Fairies

Alven

Fairies from Holland who are attached to bodies of water: lakes, rivers, and ponds. They get inside bubbles and float along to their destination and sometimes they take the shapeshifted form of an otter. They come out in hordes on the full moon, dancing and playing in the moonlight reflected on the water.

Dryad

Originally a Greek type of tree fairy. But truly their presence is worldwide. If you listen to the trees blowing in the breeze, you’ll hear their whispers. Dryads appear as pure whisps of light that dart from tree-top to tree-top. Dryads may be the spirits who gave the name to the Celtic priesthood, Druids.

Jimaninos

They are trooping fairies that fly in groups over Mexico particularly on holy nights of the year. When seen, they look like cupids or fat, chubby children. There are both male and female. This is where the theory of fairies being unbaptized children’s souls is perpetuated. Since the jimaninos resemble children and fly in the sky, people who see the assume they are lost children’s souls.

Knockers

They tend to make a lot of noise and they are heard deep in the earth – in caves and mines. They’ve scared off many miners over the centuries others claim knockers lead the miners to riches. Knockers can create mine cave-ins but have also helped lost miners find their way out. The custom is to knock when you enter the mine to let them know you’re there and doff your hat in greeting.

Pixies

Pixies are the most well-known type of fairy and seem to be the image most people call up when hearing the word “fairy”. Tinkerbell, the fairy in Peter Pan, is a pixie and depicts the classic piskie looks: small, winged creature with a large head and tiny body. Pixies love gardens full of flowers and are active on the Spring and Summer. They are generally helpful but incredibly mischievous.

Callicantzaroi

They are malevolent fairies that ride on the backs of chickens and their favorite food is pork. They are supposedly ugly little men who ride in groups. The Callicantzaroi are active on the nights approaching winter solstice and supposedly are never seen past the twelfth night. Illustrations show the callicantzaros having misformed teeth, donkey ears and goat legs. In Turkey they are equated with vampires and werewolves.

Banshee

They are believed to be attached to the old noble Irish families. Her scream is heard right before someone dies, right outside the dying’s window. If you see her washing bloody shrouds in the river, it’s a sign that a family member will soon depart, therefore many people fear the presence of this type of fairy.

Chin-Chin

They are a Japanese house fairies that prefers the home to be kept clean. Specifically the floors. If it’s clean, they will bless the home. If it’s not clean and the residents are lazy, this fairy will pick on the home’s inhabitants with no regard. They look like tiny, wizened elves but are surprisingly energetic and spry.
